Assignment

For the end-of-course project, you’ll be applying the knowledge and skills gained throughout this semester to conduct and share the social network analysis you proposed in Unit 5 Part 1. Similar to the case studies provided in this course, your final project should demonstrate your ability to wrangle, analyze, and communicate findings in response to a research question of interest.

Your findings can be shared in a format of your choosing (e.g., HTML, PDF, MS Word, slides, Tufte-style handouts, dashboards, shiny app, research articles, etc.) and your analysis and findings should be tailored to your intended audience (e.g., teachers, district admin, policymakers, researchers). You will also be expected to use R and R Markdown (or Quarto if preferred) to conduct your analysis and create a reproducible data product. Your R project should contain all the code used during each step of your analysis so it can be replicated by others, but depending on your audience, you may choose to not display your code in your data product.

When you are ready to share your final case study, please click “Add a new discussion topic” to create a new forum post. In your post, include a link to your data product, and if you worked in RStudio desktop, also attach or link to the associated R markdown file use to create you case study. If your data is sensitive or proprietary and not suitable for including in your post, please let me know.

Grading Criteria

Grading criteria for your final project is guided by the Data-Intensive Research Workflow from Learning Analytics Goes to School (Krumm, Means, and Bienkowski 2018) and Social Network Analysis and Education (Carolan 2014). Your final project is worth 12 points and the grading criteria is detailed below.

Prepare (3 points total; 1 points each)

Your case study should demonstrate your ability to formulate clear, actionable research questions relevant to a targeted audience. Specifically, your case study should include an introduction that:

  1. Clearly describes the purpose of your case study, including the research question(s) guiding your analyses.

  2. Provides information about the data sources, including the context in which the data was collected and variables used to address your research questions.

  3. Includes a description of the target audience that would most benefit from your analysis and why.

Wrangle (3 points total; 1 points each)

Your data project should demonstrate your ability to use R to manipulate, clean, transform, and/or merge relational data in order to prepare your network analysis. Specifically, your case study should clearly describe: 

  1. Data preprocessing steps (e.g., renaming variables, filtering, dichotomization, recoding values, etc.) taken to clean and prepare your text for analysis and why are they necessary.

  2. Any new variables or features you created (e.g., degree , reciprocity, etc.) to address your research questions. 

  3. Data transformations you made required for the types of analyses or packages used (e.g. reshaping, merging, joining, matrix conversions, etc.).  

Analyze (3 points total; 1 points each)

Your data project should demonstrate your ability to use R to apply network analysis techniques appropriate to your research questions and data sources. Specifically, your case study shouldclearly include:   

  1. Clear descriptions of the exploratory techniques and/or models (e.g., level of analysis, community detection, ERGMs, etc.) you applied to analyze your data.

  2. Interpretations/meanings of specific metrics (e.g., counts, means, centrality measures, etc.) that your analysis generated. 

  3. Data visualizations that are attractive and easy to interpret, and include text or narrative for aiding their Interpretation by others.

Communication (3 points total; 1 points each)

Your case study should demonstrate your ability to clearly and effectively communicate the most important and most useful findings. Specifically, your case study should:

  1. Highlights key finding and insights in response to your research questions. 

  2. Suggests potential action that you and/or your target audience to better understand and improve the context in which your data product is intended to inform.  

  3. Discusses the limitations and any ethical/legal issues taken into consideration, including acknowledgments any reference materials used.
